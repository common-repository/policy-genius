== Installation ==


You can install Policy Genius via the plugin directory, or by uploading the files manually to your server. 

1. Upload the plugin folder to the /wp-content/plugins/ directory or install via the Add New Plugin menu
2. Activate the plugin through the Plugins menu in WordPress
3. In the Settings section, open Policy Guru.
4. Fill in all data for cookie notification and save changes.
5. Fill in all data for the privacy policy and save changes.
6. Create a "New Post" or "New Page" through your WordPress Dashboard
7. Enter the shortcode in the visual or text editor, exactly as written below:
[privacy_policy]
8. Privately publish the post or page. This can be done through selecting the "Private" option under "Visibility"
9. The following page is your custom Privacy Policy. You may copy the text and post it as your website's Privacy Policy.

== Short Code ==
Once the plugin settings are installed, place the following **Shortcode** into any page or post **[privacy_policy]**

== Frequently Asked Questions ==

1. Is this Privacy Policy a legal document?  
No. This is a free policy template that takes into account the data protection requirements and best practices, as well as providing notification to users regarding a websites' personal information handling practices, created by privacy experts. However, the policy is not a legal document - please consult a lawyer if necessary. 

2. What is a Privacy Policy?  
A Privacy Policy (or "Privacy Statement" or "Privacy Notice") notifies users of an organization's personal information handling practices. It is intended to disclose how the organization collects, uses, stores, transfers, and disposes of personal information, along with how it obtains consent and how users can access said information. Privacy Policies are a legal requirement in certain countries. 

3. Why do I need a Privacy Policy?   
Depending on your location, having a Privacy Policy may be a legal requirement of your jurisdiction's privacy law. Furthermore, having a Privacy Policy is just best practice for transparency and openness. It is informational for site visitors, and also holds organizations accountable to protect data.

4. Where should I post my Privacy Policy?  
It is recommended that site visitors be able to find your organization's Privacy Policy through a link on the website home page. This is often found at the bottom of the front page.

4. Is the plugin Free?
[KI Design](https://kidesign.io) has provided a free version of the plugin. An advanced version can be accessed from http://privacypolicy.guru
